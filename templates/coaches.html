Johnnie Williams

Head Coach · Sprints & Relays

Born in Seattle, WA, Williams began his Track and Field career at age 5 in the Seattle Parks and Recreation track program. He is currently the EWU record holder in the 55m, 60m, and 200m indoor, as well as the 100m and 200m outdoor. Coach Williams is also the Athletic Director for Seattle Parks and Recreation, bringing institutional knowledge and elite competitive experience to every practice.

Kathleen Collins

Sprints & Hurdles

Started coaching at High Point Community Center when she moved to West Seattle in 2007. It was there she met and coached alongside Johnnie Williams before joining High Voltage in 2008. Kathleen focuses on the 400 meters — "the best race in track" — and still holds her Jr. High School record for the 100 yards.

Howard Kellogg

Throws

Specialist in shot put, discus, javelin, and hammer. Coach Kellogg brings technical precision and competitive depth to the throws program, developing athletes for both regional and national championships.

Jeanine Shepherd

Sprints

A dedicated sprint coach who works closely with developing athletes on start mechanics, acceleration phases, and race tactics across the short sprint events.

Jake B.

Distance

Leads the distance program with a focus on pacing strategy, aerobic base building, and developing mental toughness across the 800m, 1500m, and 3000m events.

Wurrie Njadoe

Jumps

Specialist in horizontal and vertical jumps — long jump, triple jump, high jump, and pole vault. Coach Njadoe develops athletes' explosive power and technical approach to produce consistent national-level performers.
